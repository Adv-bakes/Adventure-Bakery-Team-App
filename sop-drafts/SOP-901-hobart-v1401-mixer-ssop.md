## Purpose

To clean and sanitize the Hobart V-1401 mixer after production so it is safe and ready for the next
day's batch.

## Scope

The mixer, its bowl, and its agitators (paddle, whip, dough arm). Cleaning is done at the end of every
production day the mixer is used, and again between products when the allergens differ.

Operating the mixer is **SOP-501**. Lubrication and repairs are Maintenance, not sanitation.

## Responsibility

- **Sanitation / Production staff** — perform the cleaning and complete FRM-901.
- **Supervisor** — checks the machine before the next run and signs the release on FRM-901.

## Procedure

### Two rules before you start

1. **Unplug the mixer before wiping it.**
2. **Never hose or pressure-wash the mixer.** Damp cloth only. Water driven into the electrics or the
   transmission will destroy the machine — this is the manufacturer's own instruction.

### 1. The machine

1. Lower the bowl.
2. **Unplug the power.**
3. Remove the bowl and the agitator (see sections 2 and 3).
4. **Dry wipe** the whole machine — top, levers and controls, column, bowl yoke and support, down to
   the footers. Get the loose flour and dried batter off before any water.
5. **Wet wipe** with a damp cloth and detergent, same coverage, top down.
6. Wipe off the detergent with a second cloth and clean water, and **let it air dry.**
7. Check the **drip cup** under the planetary is clean and dry. If there is oil in it, don't run the
   machine — tell the Supervisor and Maintenance. *(It sits directly over the bowl, so oil there can
   get into product.)*
8. Plug the mixer back in once everything is dry and reassembled.

### 2. The bowl

1. Break it down and remove all residual ingredients.
2. Rinse with warm water.
3. Scrub with detergent.
4. Empty out.
5. Run through the **pan washer** on the high-temperature cycle.
6. Store clean, dry and inverted.

> The pan washer's hot final rinse is what sanitizes the bowl — so the cycle temperature matters.
> If the washer is down, wash and sanitize the bowl by hand in the sink, the same way as the
> agitators in section 3.

### 3. The paddle, whip and dough arm

Washed in the sink:

1. **Detergent** wash.
2. **Rinse.**
3. **Sanitizing solution** at label strength.
4. Onto the **drying rack** — let them air dry. Don't towel them off; that undoes the sanitizing.

Check the sanitizer strength with a test strip and write the reading on FRM-901.

### 4. Changing between products with different allergens

Do the full clean above, then **look over every food contact surface under good light** before the
next product goes in — bowl inside and under the rim, the agitator including the wire crossings and
the shank, and the agitator shaft. Anything you can see, re-clean.

Record it on FRM-901.

### 5. Before the next run

The Supervisor checks and signs FRM-901:

- Bowl, agitator and machine clean and dry
- Drip cup clean, no oil
- No cloths, tools or anything else left on the machine
- Sanitizer strength recorded

**The mixer isn't ready for production until that's signed.**
