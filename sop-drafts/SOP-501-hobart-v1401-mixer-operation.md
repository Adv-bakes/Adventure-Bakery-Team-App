## Purpose

To run the Hobart V-1401 mixer safely and get a consistent batch.

**This mixer has no bowl guard.** The agitator is exposed whenever the bowl is up, and it will take a
hand off. Every rule below about staying out of the bowl is there because nothing else will stop you.

## Scope

Production staff operating, loading, or unloading the mixer.

Cleaning is **SOP-901**. Lubrication, agitator clearance, and repairs are Maintenance — not operators.

## Responsibility

- **Operators** — run the mixer as written; stop and report anything unusual.
- **Supervisor** — trains and signs off operators; takes the machine out of service on a fault.
- **Maintenance** — all lubrication, adjustment, and repair, with the power locked out.

## Procedure

### Before you start

**Don't operate this mixer unless the Supervisor has trained and signed you off.**

Check, with the machine stopped:

- FRM-903 is signed for this shift — if it isn't, the mixer hasn't been released for production
- Drip cup is in place, clean, and **dry — no oil**
- Bowl and agitator are clean and undamaged; no cracks, no loose whip wires
- Available aprons and covers are on; controls and buttons work; oil gauge is between the middle and
  the top
- Floor is dry and clear

Anything fails → don't run it. Tag it and tell the Supervisor.

**Get yourself ready:** sleeves down and secured, no loose apron ties, no dangling lanyards, **no
rings, watches or bracelets**, hair fully in a hairnet. Anything that can be caught, will be.

### 1. Fit the bowl — bowl first, always

1. Clutch handle to **STOP**, press **STOP**.
2. Lower the bowl lift all the way down.
3. Bring the bowl in on the bowl truck — **don't carry it.**
4. Square alignment bracket to the **back**; locating studs into the holes in the bowl.
5. Raise the yoke until the bowl seats.
6. **Lock both bowl clamps** over the ears. Both. A clamp that isn't closed lets the bowl move into
   the agitator.

### 2. Fit the agitator

With the bowl in and fully lowered, push the agitator shank up onto the shaft and **turn it clockwise
until it latches**. Tug down to check. An unlatched agitator drops into the batch.

### 3. Load

Charge ingredients **with the agitator stopped** — clutch at STOP, STOP button pressed.

Check the batch sheet's allergens against the last product run. If they differ, the mixer needs an
allergen changeover clean under SOP-901 first.

Keep pens, tape, thermometers, and spare utensils away from the open bowl.

### 4. Mix

1. Clutch handle at **STOP**
2. Set the **speed** from the batch sheet
3. Set the **timer** (or **HOLD** for untimed) — *for under 3 minutes, turn past 3 and back*
4. Press **START**
5. Move the clutch handle to **RUN**

To stop: clutch to **STOP**, then press **STOP**.

**To change speed mid-batch:** clutch to **STOP** → wait for the agitator to stop completely → move
the selector, **seated on a number, never between numbers** → clutch back to **RUN**. Shifting under
load wrecks the transmission.

| Speed | Use |
|-------|-----|
| **1 — Low** | Dough, heavy batters. Also for working flour in without throwing dust. |
| **2 — Medium-Low** | Cake batters; developing dough. |
| **3 — Medium-High** | Working air into light batches. |
| **4 — High** | Maximum aeration, light batches only. |

**Never start a full bowl in 3 or 4.** Start low, bring it together, then step up.

### 5. While it's running

- **Never put a hand, scraper, cloth, or thermometer into a moving bowl.** To scrape down or sample:
  clutch **STOP** → press **STOP** → wait until it has completely stopped → then reach in.
- **Never leave it running unattended.**
- Don't lean over the open bowl.
- Stand clear of the bowl yoke when the bowl moves under power — it's a crush point.
- Stop immediately on any odd noise, vibration, smell, or smoke.

### 6. Unload

1. Clutch to **STOP**, press **STOP**, wait for it to stop completely
2. Lower the bowl
3. Remove the agitator
4. **Unlock both clamps before lowering the bowl onto the truck**
5. Hand over to sanitation — **SOP-901**

## If something goes wrong

| Problem | What to do |
|---------|-----------|
| Won't start | Check the speed selector is seated on a number. Otherwise it's the power supply or an overload — get Maintenance. |
| Stalls under load | Press **STOP**. Reduce the batch or clear the obstruction. **Don't restart against a stall in a lower speed.** |
| Agitator touching the bowl | Stop. Check both clamps are closed. If it still touches, tag it out and get Maintenance — **and hold the batch**, there may be metal in it. |
| Squealing from the planetary | Tell Maintenance. Don't oil it yourself. |
| **Oil in the drip cup or on the shaft** | **Stop. Tag it out. Tell Quality and Maintenance.** Hold any product mixed since the last clear check. |

## Open Safety Item — No Bowl Guard

> Hobart's later manual for this same model says the mixer is not to be used without the interlocked
> guard — on the guarded version the machine won't even run unless the cage is closed and the bowl is
> up. This machine has neither.
>
> OSHA's policy on vertical food mixers applies §1910.212 here and holds that **work practices are not
> accepted instead of a guard where guarding is feasible.** A retrofit guard with the interlock switch
> is sold for this model (Hobart `00-875820`), so it is feasible.
>
> The rules in sections 3 and 5 reduce the risk in the meantime. **They are not a substitute for a
> guard.** Fitting one, or replacing the machine, is a management decision tracked outside this SOP.
