## Who this is for
Production-floor employees of **Adventure Bakery**, a B2B **contract-manufacturing industrial
bakery** pursuing SQF (Safe Quality Food) certification. Tone: warm, confident, people-first — but
the setting is a food **plant**, not a shop. This module is practical and hands-on: it's the
everyday hygiene habits the floor must live by.

## Hard constraints (do not break these)
1. **Exactly 12 slides, in the order below.** Our import tooling maps narration to slides by
   position — do not add, remove, or reorder slides.
2. Slides are converted to **960×540 PNG images** for an in-app viewer. Everything must stay
   legible at that size: body text ≥ ~16 pt equivalent, high contrast, no fine hairline detail.
3. **Fonts:** stick to widely available fonts (the conversion server substitutes exotic ones).
4. **Never name customers, retailers, or branded products.** If you reference products, use
   "our plant-based burgers" / "our rum cakes". No real retailer or brand names on slides.
5. **Industrial imagery only:** hand-wash stations, gowning/locker areas, workers in
   **bouffant caps/hairnets, beard nets, smocks and aprons**, foot-baths/boot-wash, color-coded
   production zones, QA clipboards. **Never** storefronts, cafés, chef hats, or retail-shop scenes.
6. Don't write speaker notes — our tooling injects the approved narration afterwards.
7. Export the final result as **PPTX**.

## The 12 slides (title · on-slide text · what the visual should communicate)

1. **Module 2 — Personal Hygiene & GMPs** · subtitle "SQF Food Safety Training" · Hero title slide;
   industrial bakery energy (hand-wash station / gowning area motif). Match Module 1's title style.
2. **Learning Objectives** · "By the end of this module you will be able to:" with four points:
   ① Explain what GMPs are and why they protect our food ② Wash and sanitize your hands the right
   way, at the right times ③ Follow our attire, jewelry, nail, and illness rules ④ Move between
   zones without spreading contamination · Four objective cards/blocks with iconography.
3. **What are GMPs?** · "The everyday rules that keep our food safe." G = Good, M = Manufacturing,
   P = Practices. Caption: "Basic conditions and habits an auditor expects to see on the floor,
   every single shift." · Acronym treatment (mirror the S·Q·F slide from Module 1).
4. **Why hygiene comes first** · "You are part of the process." Message chip: "Clean people make
   safe food." · A team of plant workers in hairnets/smocks — positive, diverse; the idea that
   people are the first line of defense against contamination.
5. **When to wash your hands** · "Wash at every one of these moments — no exceptions." Four checks:
   before starting & after every break · after the restroom · after handling trash, cleaning, or
   touching face/hair · whenever you switch tasks or change zones · Checklist treatment.
6. **How to wash — every time** · "The single most important habit in the plant." Five-step
   sequence: Wet with warm water → Soap & scrub 20 seconds (backs, between fingers, nails) →
   Rinse clean → Dry with a paper towel → Sanitize. **Emphasize the 20-second scrub step.** ·
   Numbered step flow at a hand-wash sink.
7. **Dress for the job** · "Attire keeps you — and the product — protected." Four points:
   hair fully covered (hairnet + beard net if needed) · clean company uniform/smock, changed when
   soiled · apron and sleeve covers where required · street clothing covered, no loose threads or
   buttons · Gowned worker / attire callout diagram.
8. **Jewelry, nails & personal items** · "If it can fall off or hide dirt, it stays out."
   Two-sided comparison: LEFT (amber) "Leave out of production" — rings/watches/bracelets/earrings,
   false nails/nail polish, phones & loose personal items · RIGHT (green) "Keep covered &
   controlled" — cuts covered with a blue metal-detectable bandage, a glove over a covered cut,
   only items allowed by policy · Footer chip: "When in doubt, leave it out."
9. **Report when you're sick** · "Vomiting, diarrhea, fever, jaundice, or an infected cut or sore?
   Tell your supervisor before you start — you may be reassigned or sent home. **You will never be
   in trouble for reporting — it keeps our food and your teammates safe.**" · The emotional anchor
   of the module — make it land (a REPORT/alert visual). Use the safe-green for the reassurance line.
10. **Footwear & foot-bath sanitation** · "Keep what's on the floor from reaching the food." Four
    points: wear the dedicated plant footwear/boots provided · step through the foot-bath/sanitizing
    station when entering · keep boots clean, never wear outside shoes on the line · report a
    foot-bath that looks empty or dirty · Foot-bath / boot-wash station motif.
11. **Move the right way between zones** · "Don't carry contamination from one area to another."
    Two-sided: LEFT (green) "Higher-care zones" — dedicated tools and gowning, enter clean (wash &
    sanitize first) · RIGHT (amber) "General production & warehouse" — different stage, different
    tools, don't backtrack into clean areas · Footer chip: "Follow the flow — clean to dirty,
    never back." · Directional zone-flow diagram.
12. **Summary** · Checklist: ✓ GMPs are the everyday habits that keep our food safe ✓ Wash hands
    the right way, at every key moment ✓ Correct attire, no jewelry or polish, report illness
    ✓ Move clean-to-dirty and never carry contamination between zones · Closing chip:
    "Next: Allergens — Know the Risk".
